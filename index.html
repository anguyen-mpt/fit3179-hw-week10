Chilren health development in Victoria by Local Government Areas (2021)

The visualisations in this page aim to explore how the children health development in Victoria varies by Local Government Area (LGA) and whether it is linked to social advantage observed in such LGAs.

Vulnerable health development of Victorian children by LGA

Source: Australian Early Development Census, 2022

From Figure 1, it can be seen that the health development score varies greatly across Victoria, with more children in regional Local Government Areas suffering from vulnerable health development than those in Greater Melbourne.

Social advantage and children health development

Source: Australian Bureau of Statistics, 2023

Socio-Economic Indexes for Areas (SEIFA) is a product developed by the ABS that ranks areas in Australia according to relative socio-economic advantage and disadvantage. Here, the SEIFA index used is Index of Relative Socio-economic Disadvantage (IRSD) which only measures of relative disadvantage. A low score indicates relatively greater disadvantage in general and vice versa.

The bubble plot in Figure 2 shows that there is a negative correlation between social advantage and vulnerable health development of children. Those LGAs that have high SEIFA score tend to have low proportion of children suffering from vulnerable health development and vice versa.
